About Burhanpur

Burhanpur, located in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh, is a historically significant city with a rich cultural heritage. Nestled on the banks of the Tapti River, it was established in the 15th century by the Faruqi dynasty and later became an important Mughal outpost. The city's strategic location made it a prominent center for trade and administration during various dynastic reigns.

Burhanpur boasts an architectural marvel, the monumental Asirgarh Fort, which stands as a testament to its historical importance. This fortification, perched atop a hill, offers panoramic views of the surrounding region. Additionally, the city is known for its grand mosques, such as the Jama Masjid and Shahi Qila Mosque, showcasing stunning Mughal architectural styles.

The city also holds cultural diversity in high regard, with a blend of Hindu, Islamic, and Marathi influences evident in its festivals, cuisine, and traditions. The Tapti River and its verdant surroundings provide a serene backdrop, enhancing the city's natural beauty. Overall, Burhanpur remains a captivating destination for history enthusiasts and cultural explorers alike.

Things To Do

1. Visit Asirgarh Fort:

Explore this imposing hilltop fort, which offers panoramic views of Burhanpur and its surroundings. It's a historical gem with architectural marvels and a rich past.

2. Jama Masjid:

Marvel at the grandeur of this Mughal-era mosque, known for its impressive architecture and intricate detailing. The tranquil atmosphere adds to its allure.

3. Shahi Qila Mosque:

Another architectural marvel, this mosque is a testament to the city's Mughal heritage. Its intricate designs and serene ambiance make it a must-visit.

4. Ahukhana:

Explore the Ahukhana, a mausoleum complex that houses the tombs of Faruqi and Mughal rulers. The intricate carvings and peaceful surroundings provide a glimpse into the city's historical legacy.

5. Dargah-e-Hakimi:

Pay a visit to this revered Sufi shrine, which holds spiritual significance for many devotees. The tranquil ambiance and beautiful architecture make it a peaceful place for reflection.

6. Siddi Sayyed Ki Dargah:

This is another prominent Sufi shrine in Burhanpur, known for its spiritual importance and architectural beauty. It's a place of worship and contemplation.

7. Explore the Local Bazaars:

Wander through the bustling markets of Burhanpur, where you can shop for traditional textiles, handicrafts, and local specialties. Don't miss out on trying the local cuisine.

8. Attend Festivals:

If you're lucky to be in Burhanpur during a festival, don't miss the opportunity to experience the vibrant celebrations and cultural traditions.

9. Tapti River Ghat:

Spend a peaceful evening along the banks of the Tapti River. It's an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll, photography, or simply enjoying the natural beauty.

10. Gurudwara Bari Sangat:

Pay your respects at this Sikh temple, known for its religious significance and peaceful ambiance. It's a place of worship and community gathering.

Remember to respect the local customs and traditions while exploring these attractions. Enjoy your visit to Burhanpur!

How To Reach

Burhanpur, located in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India, can be reached via various modes of transportation. Here are the most common ways to reach Burhanpur:

1. By Air:

- The nearest airport to Burhanpur is Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Airport (IATA: IDR) in Indore, which is approximately 210 kilometers away. From there, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Burhanpur.

2. By Train:

- Burhanpur has its own railway station, known as Burhanpur Railway Station (IRCTC Code: BAU). It is well-connected to major cities like Mumbai, Bhopal, Indore, and others. You can check train schedules and book tickets through the Indian Railways' official website or at the station.

3. By Road:

- Burhanpur is well-connected by road to major cities in Madhya Pradesh and neighboring states. You can either drive or take a bus to reach Burhanpur. - If you're coming from Indore, you can take NH47 and NH59, which will take approximately 4-5 hours by road.

4. By Bus:

- There are regular bus services connecting Burhanpur to various cities in Madhya Pradesh and neighboring states. You can find both government and private bus operators providing services to Burhanpur.

5. By Car:

- If you prefer a more personalized mode of transportation, you can hire a taxi or drive your own car. The road network leading to Burhanpur is generally well-maintained.

Remember to check for any updates or changes in transportation options, especially if you're traveling during holidays or special events. It's always a good idea to confirm schedules and availability before your journey.

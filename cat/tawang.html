About Tawang

Tawang, nestled in the lap of the Himalayas in Arunachal Pradesh, is a breathtaking destination known for its awe-inspiring natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and spiritual significance. This pristine region is famous for the Tawang Monastery, one of the largest Tibetan Buddhist monasteries outside of Tibet. The monastery is perched atop a hill and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.

Tawang is blessed with stunning landscapes, including lush green valleys, serene lakes, and snow-capped peaks. The stunning Sela Pass is a highlight, offering jaw-dropping vistas and pristine lakes. The Nuranang Waterfall, also known as the Jang Waterfall, is another enchanting sight in the area.

The region is home to the Monpa people, who have a rich cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors can explore the local way of life, indulge in traditional Monpa cuisine, and partake in festivals that celebrate their culture.

Tawang is a paradise for trekkers and adventure enthusiasts, offering numerous trekking trails that wind through dense forests, remote villages, and scenic vistas. Whether you seek spiritual solace, cultural immersion, or the thrill of adventure, Tawang has it all, making it a truly remarkable destination in the northeastern frontier of India.

Things To Do

Tawang, in Arunachal Pradesh, is a destination that offers a wide range of activities for travelers seeking natural beauty, cultural experiences, and adventure. Here are some of the things to do in Tawang:

1. Visit Tawang Monastery: Explore the stunning Tawang Monastery, one of the largest and most significant Tibetan Buddhist monasteries outside of Tibet. Marvel at its intricate architecture and spiritual ambiance.

2. Sela Pass: Drive through the breathtaking Sela Pass, located at a high altitude of over 13,000 feet. Enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding snow-capped peaks and pristine lakes, including Sela Lake.

3. Nuranang Waterfall: Visit the Nuranang Waterfall, also known as Jang Waterfall, which is a mesmerizing natural wonder surrounded by lush greenery.

4. Trekking: Tawang offers several trekking opportunities for both beginners and experienced trekkers. Popular trekking routes include the Bum La Pass trek, Shonga-tser Lake trek, and more.

5. Madhuri Lake: Explore Madhuri Lake (Sangetsar Lake), famous for its crystal-clear waters and scenic surroundings. The lake gained recognition after being featured in a Bollywood movie.

6. Tawang War Memorial: Pay your respects at the Tawang War Memorial, dedicated to the soldiers who sacrificed their lives during the 1962 Indo-Sino War. The memorial also offers panoramic views of the valley.

7. Local Cuisine: Savor traditional Monpa cuisine and other regional specialties. Try dishes like thukpa, momos, and butter tea at local eateries.

8. Cultural Immersion: Interact with the Monpa community and learn about their culture and traditions. Visit local villages, participate in festivals, and experience their warm hospitality.

9. Adventure Sports: Engage in adventure sports like paragliding, river rafting, and angling in the Tawang-Chu River.

10. Yak Safari: Enjoy a yak safari to explore the picturesque landscapes. Yaks are an integral part of the local culture.

11. Photography: Tawang's stunning landscapes and vibrant culture offer excellent photography opportunities. Capture the natural beauty and cultural richness of the region.

Tawang is a destination that offers a perfect blend of spirituality, natural beauty, and adventure. Whether you're interested in exploring ancient monasteries, embarking on treks, or simply soaking in the serene Himalayan landscapes, Tawang has something to offer for every type of traveler.

How To Reach

Reaching Tawang in Arunachal Pradesh is an adventurous journey that involves navigating through the Himalayan terrain and remote regions. Tawang is located at a high altitude and can be reached by road, which is the most common way to access the region. Here's how you can reach Tawang:

By Road:

- The most popular route to Tawang is from Tezpur in Assam. First, reach Tezpur by air or train, which is relatively well-connected. From Tezpur, drive to Tawang, covering a distance of approximately 320 kilometers. The journey usually takes around 10-12 hours, and it involves crossing the challenging Sela Pass.

- Alternatively, you can also reach Tawang from Guwahati, Assam. The road journey from Guwahati to Tawang is longer, covering approximately 480 kilometers, and it takes about 14-16 hours. The route includes Tezpur, Bhalukpong, and Bomdila.

It's essential to be well-prepared for this road trip, as it involves steep mountain roads, high altitudes, and varying weather conditions. It's advisable to hire a local driver who is experienced with the terrain. Moreover, obtaining the required permits to enter Arunachal Pradesh is mandatory for Indian and foreign tourists, so ensure you have the necessary paperwork before your journey.

By Air:

- There is an airport in Tawang, the Tawang Airport (Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati to Tawang, the journey is much more comfortable by road, as air travel to Tawang is limited and subject to weather conditions.

Tawang's remote location and challenging terrain make the journey an adventure in itself, but the stunning landscapes and cultural experiences that await you in Tawang are well worth the effort.

About Shikharji

Shikharji, located in the Giridih district of Jharkhand, India, is a revered pilgrimage site for Jains and a place of great spiritual significance. Also known as Parasnath Hill, Shikharji is the highest mountain in the state of Jharkhand and a place where 20 of the 24 Jain Tirthankaras, or spiritual leaders, are believed to have attained spiritual liberation, known as Nirvana.

This sacred site is dotted with numerous Jain temples, each associated with a specific Tirthankara. The pilgrimage to Shikharji typically involves a steep ascent to the summit, where devotees offer prayers and seek spiritual enlightenment. The journey to the top is a test of faith, devotion, and physical endurance.

Shikharji's lush green surroundings, serene ambiance, and historical significance make it a place for reflection, prayer, and self-discovery. It attracts pilgrims and tourists alike, all seeking to connect with the spiritual heritage and natural beauty of the region.

Things To Do

Shikharji, a revered Jain pilgrimage site in Jharkhand, offers a unique blend of spirituality and natural beauty. Here are some things to do when visiting Shikharji:

1. Visit the Temples: Explore the various Jain temples on the hill dedicated to different Tirthankaras. Pay your respects and learn about the spiritual significance of each temple.

2. Undertake a Pilgrimage: Participate in the revered pilgrimage to the summit of Shikharji. The pilgrimage typically involves a steep ascent and offers a profound spiritual experience.

3. Enjoy the Scenic Hike: Even if you're not on a pilgrimage, you can enjoy the scenic hike to the summit. The lush green surroundings and panoramic views make it a delightful trek.

4. Bird Watching: Shikharji is home to various bird species. Birdwatchers can spot a range of avian life in the surrounding forests.

5. Meditation and Reflection: The peaceful ambiance of Shikharji is perfect for meditation and self-reflection. Find a quiet spot to connect with your inner self.

6. Photography: Capture the stunning views and the architectural beauty of the Jain temples. Remember to be respectful while taking photographs.

7. Learn About Jainism: Gain insight into Jainism, its principles, and the history of Shikharji. Engage with locals and guides to learn more about the culture.

8. Explore the Nearby Area: The region around Shikharji offers opportunities for exploring the natural beauty and local culture of Jharkhand.

9. Respect Local Customs: Be mindful of the religious customs and traditions of the site. Dress modestly and adhere to any rules or guidelines.

Shikharji is a destination that offers a harmonious blend of spiritual devotion and the natural beauty of the hill. It is a place for both pilgrims seeking spiritual growth and travelers looking to connect with the rich cultural heritage of the region.

How To Reach

Shikharji, a prominent Jain pilgrimage site in Jharkhand, is accessible by road. Here's how to reach Shikharji:

By Air:

- The nearest airport to Shikharji is Birsa Munda Airport in Ranchi, the capital of Jharkhand. After arriving at the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach Shikharji. The distance between Ranchi and Shikharji is approximately 150 kilometers, and the journey typically takes around 4-5 hours by road.

By Train:

- The nearest railway station to Shikharji is Parasnath Railway Station. Parasnath is well-connected to major cities in India. After reaching Parasnath, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach Shikharji, which is approximately 25 kilometers away.

By Road:

- Shikharji is well-connected by road. You can reach it by taking a taxi or a bus from nearby towns and cities. The most common route is from Ranchi to Shikharji, which takes around 4-5 hours by road.

- Another option is to reach the town of Madhuban, which is close to Shikharji, and then take a local vehicle or trek to the pilgrimage site.

Please note that the journey to Shikharji involves hilly terrain and may require some trekking or walking, so be prepared for the physical aspects of the pilgrimage. Additionally, Shikharji is a place of spiritual significance for Jains, so it's important to be respectful of the site's customs and traditions.

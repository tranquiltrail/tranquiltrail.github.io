About Loktak Lake

Loktak Lake, nestled in the northeastern state of Manipur, India, is a natural marvel renowned for its unique floating phumdis (masses of vegetation, soil, and organic matter). This picturesque lake spans approximately 287 square kilometers, making it the largest freshwater lake in Northeast India. The phumdis not only serve as a distinctive ecological habitat but also support a community of fishermen who live on these floating islands.

At the center of Loktak Lake lies the Keibul Lamjao National Park, which is the world's only floating national park. It is a critical habitat for the endangered Manipur brow-antlered deer, also known as the Sangai. The serene waters, dotted with colorful boats, reflect the surrounding hills and vibrant vegetation, creating a surreal panorama.

Loktak Lake is not only an ecological treasure but also holds cultural significance for the Manipuri people. The Sendra Island on the lake serves as a popular tourist spot for boat rides and leisurely strolls. The lake's sheer expanse and rich biodiversity make it a destination that seamlessly combines natural splendor with cultural heritage.

Things To Do

Loktak Lake in Manipur, India, offers a range of activities for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and those interested in local culture. Here are some things to do in Loktak Lake:

1. Boat Tour:

Explore the unique floating islands and wildlife habitats of Loktak Lake on a guided boat tour. This allows you to get up close to the lake's fascinating ecosystem.

2. Visit Keibul Lamjao National Park:

Explore the world's only floating national park, home to the endangered Manipur brow-antlered deer, locally known as Sangai. Enjoy wildlife spotting and birdwatching.

3. Bird Watching:

Loktak Lake is a haven for birdwatchers. Bring your binoculars and observe a diverse range of bird species, including migratory birds that visit the lake during the winter months.

4. Photography:

Capture the stunning scenery, including the floating phumdis, serene waters, and the surrounding hills. The reflections on the lake's surface make for picturesque shots.

5. Canoeing and Kayaking:

Rent a canoe or kayak to explore the lake at your own pace. Paddle through the calm waters and discover hidden corners of Loktak.

6. Fishing:

Engage in angling activities with the local fishermen, learning about their traditional techniques and experiencing their way of life.

7. Visit Sendra Island:

This picturesque island on Loktak Lake is ideal for picnics, leisurely walks, and taking in panoramic views of the lake.

8. Trekking:

Some parts of the lake's shore offer trekking opportunities, providing a chance to explore the surrounding hills, forests, and villages.

9. Explore Karang Island:

Visit Karang Island, known for its ancient temple and the panoramic views it offers of the lake.

10. Cultural Interaction:

Engage with the local communities around the lake to learn about their customs, traditions, and way of life. This cultural exchange can be a rich and rewarding experience.

Remember to respect the natural environment and local customs during your visit to Loktak Lake. Enjoy your time amidst the captivating beauty of Manipur!

Reaching Loktak Lake in Manipur, India involves a combination of air, rail, and road travel. Here are the steps to reach Loktak Lake: By Air:

- The nearest major airport is Imphal International Airport (also known as Bir Tikendrajit International Airport) in Imphal, Manipur. It is approximately 45 kilometers away from Loktak Lake. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the lake.

By Train:

- The closest railway station is Dimapur Railway Station in Assam, which is approximately 215 kilometers away from Loktak Lake. From Dimapur, you'll need to continue your journey by road.

By Road:

- Loktak Lake is well-connected by road to various towns and cities in Manipur. You can either hire a private taxi, take a bus, or drive your own vehicle. The roads leading to Loktak Lake offer scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

Travel Tips:

- It's recommended to check the weather conditions and road conditions before planning your trip, especially during the monsoon season. - Carry appropriate clothing, especially if you plan to go trekking or hiking around the lake. - Hire a local guide for the trek to ensure safety and to get the most out of your experience. - Arrange for accommodations and permits in advance, especially if you plan to stay overnight near Loktak Lake.

Remember that Loktak Lake is a protected area, so it's important to respect the environment and follow any regulations or guidelines provided by local authorities. Enjoy your journey to this stunning natural wonder!

About Amarkantak

Amarkantak, nestled in the eastern part of Madhya Pradesh, India, is a serene town of great religious and natural significance. Known as the "King of Pilgrimages," it is revered as the source of the Narmada, Son, and Johila rivers, making it a sacred site for Hindus. The Narmada Udgam, where the Narmada River emerges from the Amarkantak plateau, is a particularly revered spot.

Apart from its spiritual importance, Amarkantak is surrounded by lush forests and is a haven for biodiversity enthusiasts. The town is situated in the Maikal mountain range, providing stunning panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and hills.

Amarkantak also houses various temples like the Narmada Temple and Shri Yantra Mandir, drawing devotees from far and wide. The town is an excellent base for treks into the nearby forests and a starting point for exploring the picturesque surroundings. With its blend of natural beauty and spiritual sanctity, Amarkantak offers a unique and enriching experience for all who visit.

Things To Do

Amarkantak, a town of spiritual and natural significance in Madhya Pradesh, offers a range of activities for visitors to explore its serene beauty and cultural richness. Here are some things to do in Amarkantak:

1. Visit Narmada Udgam:

Witness the sacred emergence of the Narmada River at Narmada Udgam. This spot holds great spiritual importance for Hindus.

2. Explore Temples:

- Narmada Temple:

Pay your respects at this ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Narmada. It's an important pilgrimage site.

- Shri Yantra Mandir:

Visit this unique temple, known for its intricate Yantra, a geometric design used for meditation.

3. Jabalpur Kund:

Admire the natural beauty of this picturesque pond, surrounded by lush greenery. It's a peaceful spot for reflection.

4. Kapil Dhara and Dugdha Dhara:

These scenic waterfalls, surrounded by dense forests, are perfect for nature enthusiasts and picnickers.

5. Sonemuda:

This is the source of the Son River, another sacred river in Hinduism. The serene surroundings make it a peaceful place for contemplation.

6. Kabir Chabutra:

Visit this spot associated with the renowned saint-poet Kabir, where it is believed that he meditated.

7. Hike and Nature Walks:

Amarkantak is surrounded by lush forests and hills, offering excellent opportunities for hiking and nature walks. The Maikal Hills provide stunning vistas.

8. Sarvodaya Jain Temple:

Explore this beautiful Jain temple known for its architectural grandeur and peaceful ambiance.

9. Botanical Gardens:

Enjoy a leisurely stroll in the beautifully maintained botanical gardens, which showcase a variety of local flora.

10. Attend Religious Ceremonies:

If possible, attend the aarti (prayer ceremony) at the Narmada Temple or other religious events for a deeper cultural experience.

Remember to respect the religious and cultural significance of the sites you visit, and to be mindful of the natural surroundings. Enjoy your visit to Amarkantak!

How To Reach

Amarkantak, located in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India, is a serene town known for its spiritual and natural significance. Here are the ways to reach Amarkantak:

1. By Air:

- The nearest airport to Amarkantak is Dumna Airport (IATA: JLR) in Jabalpur, which is approximately 245 kilometers away. From Jabalpur, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Amarkantak.

2. By Train:

- The nearest railway station to Amarkantak is Pendra Road Railway Station (station code: PND), which is about 40 kilometers away. However, it is a relatively small station with limited train services. Anuppur Junction (station code: APR) is another option, located around 70 kilometers away and has better connectivity. From the railway station, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach Amarkantak.

3. By Road:

- Amarkantak is well-connected by road to various cities in Madhya Pradesh and neighboring states. You can either drive your own vehicle or take a bus. There are regular bus services from nearby towns and cities.

4. By Bus:

- There are regular bus services connecting Amarkantak to cities like Jabalpur, Bilaspur, and Shahdol. State-run and private bus operators provide services to Amarkantak.

5. By Car:

- If you prefer a more personalized mode of transportation, you can hire a taxi or drive your own car. The road network leading to Amarkantak is generally well-maintained.

Please note that the road leading to Amarkantak can be hilly and winding, so if you're driving, it's advisable to be cautious, especially during monsoon or in the evening. Always check for any updates or changes in transportation options before your journey. Enjoy your visit to Amarkantak!

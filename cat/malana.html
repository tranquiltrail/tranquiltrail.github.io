About Malana

Nestled in the pristine Parvati Valley of Himachal Pradesh, Malana is a remote and enigmatic village that has gained a reputation for its unique culture, traditions, and stunning natural beauty. Situated at an elevation of around 3,029 meters (9,938 feet) above sea level, Malana is often referred to as the "Little Greece of India" due to its distinct cultural and historical attributes.

The village is renowned for its strict social structure and the practice of self-isolation. It is famous for its Malana Cream, a high-quality cannabis strain grown in the region.

Malana is also home to various historical and archaeological sites, including old temples and ancient structures. The village offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Himalayan mountains, making it a trekker's paradise.

Visiting Malana offers a unique opportunity to experience an age-old culture and immerse oneself in the serenity of the Himalayan landscape, making it a destination of intrigue and natural beauty.

Things To Do

Malana, a unique and remote village in Himachal Pradesh, offers a distinct cultural and natural experience. Here are some things to do when visiting Malana:

1. Explore the Village: Wander through the village and admire its traditional stone architecture, narrow pathways, and ancient temples. Malana has a unique atmosphere and is known for its distinctive customs.

2. Learn About the Culture: Interact with the locals and learn about the unique customs and traditions of the Malana people. The village has a strict social structure and maintains its ancient culture.

3. Hike and Trek: Malana is a starting point for various treks, including the Chandrakhani Pass and the Malana-Chanderkhani Pass trek. These treks offer stunning views of the Himalayan landscape.

4. Enjoy the Scenic Beauty: Malana is surrounded by picturesque landscapes. Take in the breathtaking views of the mountains, lush forests, and the serene Parvati Valley.

5. Visit Ancient Temples: Explore the village's ancient temples, including the Jamdagni Temple and the Renuka Temple, which provide insight into the region's history and spirituality.

6. Photography: Capture the unique architecture, landscape, and people of Malana. Please be respectful and seek permission before taking photographs of locals.

7. Taste Local Food: Try local dishes in the village's few eateries, such as traditional Himachali cuisine. Malana also produces its distinct type of potatoes.

8. Respect Local Customs: Be aware of and respectful of the village's customs and traditions. Do not touch religious structures or objects without permission.

9. Cannabis Culture: Malana is known for its cannabis culture. While it is grown in the region, be aware that its use is subject to local laws and regulations.

10. Pack Light: Travel light as there are no vehicles in the village, and everything must be carried on foot.

Malana offers a one-of-a-kind cultural and natural experience, making it a destination for those seeking to explore unique traditions and the pristine beauty of the Himalayas. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and maintain the area's natural purity.

How To Reach

Reaching Malana, a remote village in Himachal Pradesh, can be an adventurous journey. Malana is not connected by a road, so the final leg of the journey requires a trek. Here's how to reach Malana:

By Air:

- The nearest airport to Malana is Bhuntar Airport, also known as Kullu-Manali Airport, in Kullu. After landing at Bhuntar Airport, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach Jari, a village located near Malana. The distance between Bhuntar Airport and Jari is approximately 30 kilometers.

By Train:

- The nearest railway station to Malana is Joginder Nagar Railway Station, which is connected to Pathankot. After reaching Joginder Nagar, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to Bhuntar or Kullu. From Bhuntar or Kullu, proceed to Jari.

By Road:

- You can reach the village of Jari by road. Jari is accessible by car or bus from nearby towns like Bhuntar, Kullu, and Manali. The distance between Bhuntar and Jari is about 32 kilometers, and it takes around 1-2 hours by road.

Trek to Malana:

- Once you reach Jari, you need to trek to Malana. The trek to Malana from Jari is approximately 4-5 kilometers and takes about 3-4 hours. The trek can be moderately challenging, and you will traverse through the scenic Parvati Valley.

Please note that Malana is a unique village with its own customs and traditions. Visitors are advised to respect the local culture, avoid touching religious structures, and seek permission before taking photographs of locals. Also, check the current conditions of the trek, as it may vary depending on the season and weather.
